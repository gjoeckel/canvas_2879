Guide

Color Reliance

Color reliance is using hue, shade, tint or tone as the only means of conveying information. Color reliance impacts users with color vision deficiency, and blind users; and it may impact users with low vision.

Objectives

  • Gain an understanding of color reliance and its impact on some users.
  • Review three best practices for avoiding color reliance.

Color Vision Deficiency

In the U.S., approximately 8% of men, and .1% of women—or about 4% of the population—have color vision deficiency, commonly called "color blindness". This condition makes it difficult to differentiate certain color combinations.

There are seven official diagnoses of color blindness. Four different types of color blindness fall into the red-green category. There are two types in the blue-yellow spectrum, and in one type people completely lack the ability to perceive color. Red-green color deficiency is the most common type.

Other Issues with Color

While it may seem obvious once you think about it, if someone is blind, they are also color blind. Screen reading software doesn't typically indicate colors in documents.

Also, some users with low vision may have difficulty perceiving certain colors. They may override the colors in a document to view them in a combination that works best for them.

Text styling

In addition to relying on color alone, do NOT rely on text formatting (e.g., bold or italics) as the ONLY means of differentiating important content or information. Screen reading software does NOT typically present additional information for visual formatting applied to text.

Charts and Graphs

Spreadsheets, charts, or graphs may also contain color-reliant information. Strategies for avoiding this issue are covered in Module 5: Accessible Excel.

Three Best Practices

To summarize, our three best practices for avoiding color reliance are:

  • Separate your content.
  • Provide text labels.
  • Use images with alternative text.

By understanding the impacts of color-reliant information and implementing best practices, your documents will be optimized for users with color vision deficiency, users with low vision, and blind users. This concludes the section on Contrast & Color Reliance.
